Correlations Between Poverty and Healthcare


Using data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System, we investigated the various health risks particular demographics are facing.

In this example, we compared the rate of poverty with the percentage of people who lack healthcare. There appears to be a general correlation between these two factors: the lower the rates of poverty, the lower the rates of people who lack healthcare. However, it does not appear to be a very strong correlation as the distribution of data points throughout the chart does not fit into one clear line.

Intuitively, it makes sense that when there is more poverty, more people would lack healthcare, but statistical analysis needs to be conducted before any solid conclusions can be drawn.
